Session Policy

Fantasy reference. Professional terms.

Jeremy Hayes Arts model sessions are used to create art reference for fantasy realism, Dreamscapes, character studies, paintings, promotional material, and related visual development.

Some sessions may involve fantasy wardrobe such as loincloths, wraps, cloaks, partial armor, barbarian-style costume pieces, body-safe coverings, or temporary mock armor. Cardboard cutouts and rough costume shapes may be used to study silhouette, lighting, pose, anatomy, and composition before the finished artwork is created.

These sessions are not erotic, pornographic, or explicit. Wardrobe, props, mock armor, poses, lighting, compensation, and usage terms must be discussed and agreed to before the shoot.

Boundaries

Clear before the shoot.

Models must be 18 or older for fantasy wardrobe, minimal costume pieces, body-form reference, loincloths, wraps, partial armor, or similar attire.

The model may refuse any wardrobe, pose, prop, concept, or setup they are not comfortable with. If anything needs to change during the session, the session pauses and adjusts before continuing.

A signed agreement and release should be completed before reference images are used for artwork, portfolio display, promotion, website use, social media, behind-the-scenes content, prints, merchandise, or other approved business purposes.
